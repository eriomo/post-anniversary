The day after the anniversary — an ordinary morning —

My dearest Princess,

The anniversary is over. The date has passed. And nothing has changed — which is the thing I most need you to understand. My feelings don't live on a calendar. They don't need a milestone to exist. They just live. Every single day, quietly, without needing a reason or a reminder. The date changed. I didn't.

You reassure me in a way I don't think you fully understand yet. Not by telling me things will be fine — I've heard that from people who didn't mean it. You do it by just being there. By responding. By making it clear, over and over, without even trying, that I am not doing this alone. There have been nights I didn't tell you I was struggling, and somehow — somehow — you still found me. That's not something I take for granted. Not once. Not ever.

I want to be that for you too. That's what I keep thinking about. Not just being loved by you — but being useful to you. Being the person whose voice makes something settle in you the way yours settles in me. I want to earn that. I'm working toward it every single day, and I need you to know that I am.

You make ordinary feel like enough. I don't need a big life with you. I just need Tuesday. The quiet ones. The ones where nothing happens except that I know you're there. Those are the ones I keep. Those are the ones I'll remember longest.

You're the occasion, Princess. Not the anniversary. Not the date. Just you — showing up, every ordinary day, choosing me back — that's what I'm writing about. That's everything. That has always been everything.

Yours, on every ordinary day — Eri 💛
